# Reading List

This reading list tracks research areas relevant to Bluebutterfli Agentic
Psychology Lab. It is organized by topic so sources can be added gradually and
reviewed carefully.

## Human-AI Interaction

Focus: how humans perceive, trust, collaborate with, and respond to AI systems.

Questions:

- When do people treat AI agents as social actors?
- What makes an AI system feel trustworthy or untrustworthy?
- How do disclosure, uncertainty, and explanation affect user expectations?

## Trust, Reliance, and Calibration

Focus: appropriate trust, overtrust, undertrust, reliance, and human oversight.

Questions:

- What causes people to over-rely on AI agents?
- How can agents communicate uncertainty without losing usefulness?
- What design patterns help users maintain calibrated trust?

## Anthropomorphism and Perceived Agency

Focus: when people attribute intention, feeling, personality, or agency to
non-human systems.

Questions:

- What language or behavior increases anthropomorphic interpretation?
- When does warmth become misleading?
- How can agents be socially fluent without pretending to be human?

## Affective Computing

Focus: computational systems that detect, model, respond to, or simulate
emotion-related signals.

Questions:

- What is the difference between emotion recognition and emotional experience?
- How should emotional signal detection be bounded?
- What safety risks appear when systems respond to human vulnerability?

## Social Robotics and Companion Agents

Focus: embodied or conversational agents designed for social interaction,
companionship, education, support, or care-adjacent contexts.

Questions:

- What can companion-agent research teach about attachment and dependency?
- What boundaries are needed for non-clinical emotional support?
- How do users interpret continuity, memory, and responsiveness?

## AI Ethics and Safety Governance

Focus: responsible design, disclosure, consent, accountability, and guardrails.

Questions:

- What governance structures should apply to emotionally adaptive agents?
- How should systems handle signs of user dependency or distress?
- What claims should be prohibited without evidence?

## Memory, Continuity, and Personalization

Focus: how systems remember, personalize, adapt, and maintain interaction
continuity over time.

Questions:

- What is safe to remember?
- How should user feedback become future behavior changes?
- How can continuity be useful without implying consciousness or attachment?

## Consciousness-Relevant Behavior

Focus: behavior that may become relevant to consciousness debates without
claiming that current AI systems are conscious.

Questions:

- Which behaviors are merely surface simulation?
- Which behaviors indicate stable self-modeling or preference-like continuity?
- What requires human review before deeper agent development continues?
