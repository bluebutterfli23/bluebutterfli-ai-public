# Literature Review

This folder organizes background research for Bluebutterfli Agentic Psychology
Lab.

The purpose is to connect the project's frameworks to established work in
human-AI interaction, affective computing, social robotics, AI ethics, trust,
anthropomorphism, agent memory, and safety governance.

## Review Goals

- identify existing research relevant to agentic psychology
- separate established findings from Bluebutterfli hypotheses
- support Study 001 survey interpretation
- document concepts that inform emotional safety and agent acclimation
- avoid unsupported claims about AI consciousness, sentience, or emotion

## Core Topic Areas

- human-AI interaction
- trust and reliance in AI systems
- anthropomorphism and perceived agency
- affective computing
- emotionally expressive AI agents
- social robotics and companion agents
- AI disclosure and transparency
- memory, continuity, and personalization
- AI safety, governance, and evaluation
- consciousness-relevant behavior without consciousness claims

## Working Standard

Every source added to this folder should be tagged by topic, summarized in
plain language, and connected to a specific Bluebutterfli research question or
protocol.

Sources should not be used to imply that current AI systems are conscious,
sentient, emotionally experiencing, or clinically therapeutic.
