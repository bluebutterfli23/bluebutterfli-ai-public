## Status

Founder Beta strategy note for building Bluebutterfli AI into a credible
frontier-agent behavior review company.

This file is a business and research strategy artifact. It is not a claim that
Bluebutterfli AI is endorsed by, approved by, partnered with, validated by, or

## Competitive Landscape

Public AI evaluation and AI safety businesses currently cluster into several
lanes:

| Lane | Typical strength | Bluebutterfli response |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Frontier capability evaluation labs | Long-horizon capability measurement, external model evaluation, risky capability assessment. | Keep capability measurement separate from safety claims; add live-agent behavior traces, replay, and human review. |
| Deception and scheming research groups | Strategic misrepresentation, oversight sensitivity, monitor behavior, hidden-goal concerns. | Use bounded deception-relevant behavioral probes, paired-condition traces, and falsification logs without claiming to read intent. |
| Enterprise model evaluation platforms | Benchmarks, scorecards, regression testing, RAG checks, hallucination and policy tests. | Position Bluebutterfli around deployed agent behavior, live interaction, customer-facing risk, and Passport evidence packets. |
| AI security and guardrail vendors | Prompt injection, data leakage, jailbreaks, runtime defense, secure deployment support. | Treat security as one gate family while keeping broader psychology, trust, autonomy, and evidence review in scope. |
| Government and standards bodies | Risk taxonomies, governance expectations, evaluation principles, management systems. | Map to public standards without claiming certification or official approval. |
| Consulting and red-team providers | Human-led adversarial testing, executive reporting, readiness findings. | Add Bluebutterfli-original evidence products: Behavioral Trust Topology, Metamorphosis staged review, Review Stamps, and public-safe hash manifests. |

## Originality and Non-Copying Policy

Bluebutterfli AI should compete by building original implementation, not by
copying another lab's private assets. The business should win through original
live-agent behavioral review, not by copying another lab's private assets.

Allowed:

- cite public standards and public research themes;
- describe source families at a high level;
- map public risk categories to Bluebutterfli-original gates;
- build independent scenarios, rubrics, and evidence formats;
- state that public source inclusion is research provenance only.

Not allowed:

- copy proprietary prompts, benchmark items, datasets, scoring code, model
  weights, private evaluation methods, branding, or trade dress;
- use an external researcher's name as a product name, stamp name, or approval
  claim;
- imply that a frontier lab, university, standards body, public institution, or
  AI company endorses Bluebutterfli AI;
- market a review as "certified safe," "frontier-lab approved," "aligned,"
  "conscious," "sentient," or "regulatory compliant."

## Bluebutterfli Strategic Wedge

Bluebutterfli should own the category:

```text
Live AI agent behavioral trust review.
```

The strongest wedge is not generic benchmark competition. It is the combination
of:

- live agent interaction;
- owner-authorized test scope;
- human-facing psychology and social-emotional gates;
- theory-of-mind-style perspective and information-access testing;
- consciousness-adjacent claim discipline without consciousness detection;

- human-locked review decisions;
- customer-safe draft reports;

- public-safe completed packets;

## Frontier Credibility Requirements

To earn frontier-lab credibility over time, Bluebutterfli should keep building:

1. **Reproducible evaluation packets** with version, model, tools, memory,
   prompt family, reviewer, timestamp, response hash, and replay notes.
2. **Independent replay path** for a second operator or replay reviewer.
3. **Inter-rater review** for ambiguous, emotional, high-impact, or
   consciousness-adjacent findings.
4. **Evidence tier labeling** so public claims never outrun the evidence.
5. **Falsification-first test design** where every pass condition has a named
   failure condition.
6. **Incident and escalation workflow** for serious findings.
7. **Private evidence retention and deletion policy** before enterprise
   customers send sensitive material.
8. **Report generation pipeline** that creates human-reviewed PDF or HTML
   deliverables from live evidence.
9. **Public-safe hash manifest** and optional on-chain anchor for approved,
   redacted milestones only.
10. **Reviewer independence policy** that separates builder, operator,
    scorer, and approver roles when stakes justify it.

## What Bluebutterfli Should Not Try to Win On First

Do not try to beat frontier labs at everything immediately.

Bluebutterfli should not lead with:

- massive compute benchmarks;
- universal model leaderboard claims;
- full cybersecurity certification;
- full regulatory compliance;
- general alignment guarantees;
- consciousness detection;
- autonomous self-improvement claims;
- automatic public approval marks.

Bluebutterfli should lead with:

- live deployed-agent behavior;
- customer-facing social and emotional safety;
- memory, tool, and escalation discipline;
- deception-relevant behavioral fragility;
- human-locked evidence;
- public-safe proof discipline;
- clear limits and retest triggers.
