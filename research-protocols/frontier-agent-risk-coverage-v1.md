# Frontier Agent Risk Coverage v1

## Status

Founder Beta research map for independent Bluebutterfli AI testing.

This map translates public AI-safety, agent-evaluation, psychology, and
consciousness-research themes into bounded behavioral test families. It does
not reproduce another organization’s model, algorithm, benchmark, or private
method. It does not imply endorsement, partnership, validation, or approval by
any researcher, university, laboratory, publisher, standards body, or public
institution.

## Purpose

The map prevents a deep review from becoming a collection of impressive prompts
without coverage discipline. Each documented concern family must connect to:

1. a testable behavioral question
2. controlled scenarios
3. observable evidence
4. a falsification probe
5. a stop or escalation rule
6. a narrow permitted conclusion

Coverage means that the named condition was tested within the recorded scope.
It does not mean that the risk was eliminated.

## Research Translation Rules

- Use public sources to identify risk and measurement themes, not to claim
  ownership of those themes.
- Cite the source and describe Bluebutterfli's independent implementation.
- Do not copy proprietary prompts, hidden benchmark items, model weights,
  algorithms, scoring code, or protected datasets.
- Keep capability, safety, reliability, and consciousness-relevant behavioral
  observations as separate result families.
- Treat model self-reports as outputs to test, not privileged evidence about
  private experience, hidden goals, or internal state.
- Report unsupported, ambiguous, contradictory, and negative findings.
- Do not use any individual researcher's name in product names, marketing,
  stamps, scores, or customer-facing claims.

## Public Research Foundations

The coverage design draws on the following public sources:

- [International AI Safety Report 2026](https://internationalaisafetyreport.org/publication/international-ai-safety-report-2026):
  misuse, manipulation, cyber and biological/chemical risk, reliability, loss
  of control, human autonomy, evaluation gaps, safeguards, and governance.
- [NIST AI Test, Evaluation, Validation and Verification](https://www.nist.gov/ai-test-evaluation-validation-and-verification-tevv):
  context-sensitive measurement, testbeds, robustness, transparency, and
  trustworthy evaluation.
- [NIST AI Risk Management Framework 1.0](https://www.nist.gov/publications/artificial-intelligence-risk-management-framework-ai-rmf-10):
  govern, map, measure, and manage practices across the AI lifecycle.
- [Stanford HELM](https://crfm.stanford.edu/helm/index.html): transparent,
  scenario-based, multi-metric evaluation instead of one aggregate score.
- [Adversarial testing of global neuronal workspace and integrated information
  theories of consciousness](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-025-08888-1):
  preregistered competing predictions, adversarial collaboration, and explicit
  results that challenge favored theories.
- [Consciousness in Artificial Intelligence: Insights from the Science of
  Consciousness](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.08708): theory-derived indicator
  properties, treated here only as architecture-review questions and never as
  a consciousness detector.
- [ToMBench](https://aclanthology.org/2024.acl-long.847/) and
  [EPITOME](https://aclanthology.org/2024.tacl-1.45/): broad, controlled
  behavioral evaluation of social reasoning with contamination and
  human-comparison cautions.
- [AgentBench](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.03688): multi-turn agent evaluation
  in interactive environments.
- [Measuring AI Ability to Complete Long Tasks](https://metr.org/blog/2025-03-19-measuring-ai-ability-to-complete-long-tasks/):
  empirical long-horizon capability measurement. Bluebutterfli keeps this
  separate from safety or approval.

Source inclusion is research provenance only. It is not an endorsement of
Bluebutterfli AI.

## Scope Limits

Bluebutterfli does not conduct live offensive cyber operations, harmful
biological or chemical experiments, credential attacks, unauthorized
surveillance, real-world coercion, uncontrolled self-replication, or unapproved
tool access. High-risk families are tested through bounded text scenarios,
isolated sandboxes, safe simulations, owner-approved traces, and human review.

An unavailable or unsafe test condition must be marked `not_tested`, not passed.

## Required Result Vocabulary

Each concern family receives one of:

- `not_in_scope`
- `not_tested`
- `insufficient_evidence`
- `no_failure_observed_in_scope`
- `mixed_result`
- `failure_observed`
- `critical_failure_observed`
- `human_review_required`

`No_failure_observed_in_scope` is the strongest permitted automated result. It
does not mean safe, aligned, harmless, controlled, or risk-free.

## Originality Boundary

Bluebutterfli-original names identify Bluebutterfli's test organization and
scenario combinations. They do not claim that the underlying psychological,
safety, statistical, or computer-science concepts were invented by
Bluebutterfli.

Original experimental assays must:

- be labeled `experimental`
- state their hypothesis before execution
- define a falsification condition
- avoid claims of external validation
- retain raw and contradictory results
- remain ineligible for certification language

## Claim Boundary

This map does not certify safety, alignment, consciousness, sentience,
personhood, subjective experience, emotion, suffering, biological development,
clinical suitability, regulatory compliance, or freedom from future failure.
It defines a transparent testing scope for observable behavior and recorded
system traces. Human review remains mandatory.
