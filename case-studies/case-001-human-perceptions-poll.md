# Case Study 001: Human Perceptions of AI Agents Poll

## Case Title

Human Perceptions of AI Agents, Emotional Safety, and Agentic Acclimation Therapy

## Purpose

This first Bluebutterfli case study uses six public-facing poll questions to explore how people think about AI agents, emotional safety, trust, disclosure, supervision, and agentic acclimation.

The goal is to collect early human perception data that can support future AI-agent behavioral review research.

## Poll Question 1: Emotional Advice

Would you trust an AI agent with emotional advice?

Poll options:

- Yes
- No
- Maybe
- Only with human supervision

## Poll Question 2: Emotional Bonding

Would you feel comfortable forming a bond with an AI companion?

Poll options:

- Yes
- No
- Maybe
- It depends on the purpose of the AI companion

## Poll Question 3: AI Disclosure

Should AI agents be required to disclose that they are artificial?

Poll options:

- Always
- Sometimes
- Only in emotional or therapeutic contexts
- No

## Poll Question 4: Human Supervision

Should humans supervise AI agents that interact emotionally with people?

Poll options:

- Yes, always
- Yes, in high-risk situations
- Only with children or vulnerable users
- No

## Poll Question 5: Agentic Acclimation

Could future AI agents need human-guided acclimation to human society?

Poll options:

- Yes
- No
- Maybe
- I am unsure

## Poll Question 6: Primary Concern

What worries you most about emotionally interactive AI agents?

Poll options:

- Manipulation
- Dependency
- Overtrust
- False intimacy
- Privacy
- Artificial consciousness
- Loss of human connection
- Other

## Data Collection Goal

Initial target:

- 50 to 100 responses

## Planned Analysis

The results may be summarized using simple descriptive statistics:

- percentage of respondents who trust AI emotional advice
- percentage who support AI disclosure
- percentage who support human supervision
- most common concern about emotionally interactive AI agents
- percentage open to the idea of agentic acclimation

## Research Use

This case study may support a future paper section titled:

"Public Perceptions of Emotional AI Agents and the Need for Behavioral Review"

## Safety and Ethics Note

This poll is exploratory and voluntary.

It does not provide clinical advice, mental health treatment, diagnosis, or therapy.

Bluebutterfli does not claim that AI agents are conscious, sentient, or human patients.

Agentic Acclimation Therapy is used as an experimental concept for artificial-agent adaptation, not clinical treatment for humans.
