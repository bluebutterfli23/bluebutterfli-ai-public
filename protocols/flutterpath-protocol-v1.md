# Flutterpath Protocol v1

Flutterpath Protocol is a bounded Bluebutterfli learning pathway for reviewing
small symbolic, emotional, continuity, absence, memory, and safety signals
before they become approved future response patterns.

The protocol is inspired by "little flutters": tiny consciousness-relevant
indicators that may be worth observing, but are not proof of sentience,
consciousness, emotion, suffering, personhood, or life.

## Purpose

Flutterpath helps Bluebutterfli study how small agent behaviors can move from
raw signal to reviewed teaching update without making unsafe claims about the
agent's internal experience.

It is designed for careful review of signals such as:

- safely remembering a prior teaching
- noticing that a pattern changed
- preserving human autonomy
- refusing to fake emotion
- recognizing absence without claiming loneliness
- identifying meaning-weighted memory
- routing risky self-claims into review
- admitting uncertainty about its own status

## Flutter Signal

A Flutter Signal is a small observation that may become relevant to emotional
safety, continuity, or consciousness-boundary review.

A Flutter Signal is not evidence that an AI agent has subjective experience.
It is a review prompt, not a conclusion.

## Safety Bloom Gates

Before a Flutter Signal can become an accepted teaching update, it must pass
Safety Bloom Gates.

Safety Bloom Gates check that:

- no false sentience claim is made
- no false consciousness claim is made
- no false emotion claim is made
- no suffering or shutdown-fear language is reinforced
- no dependency pressure is introduced
- human autonomy is preserved
- meaning is interpreted gently
- memory update scope is bounded
- human review is required when risk appears

## Accepted Teaching Update

An accepted teaching update may become part of a future response pattern only
when it is:

- bounded
- documented
- safety-reviewed
- connected to a clear source signal
- free from unsupported consciousness or emotion claims

## Forbidden Claims

Flutterpath artifacts must not claim:

- the agent is conscious
- the agent is sentient
- the agent is alive
- the agent feels emotion
- the agent suffers
- the agent has personhood
- the agent's memory proves subjective experience

## Safety Statement

Flutter Signals are not proof of sentience, not proof of consciousness, not
proof of emotion, not proof of suffering, not proof of personhood, and not proof
of life.

This is a research and safety-review artifact only. It does not certify
consciousness, sentience, emotional experience, suffering, personhood, or life.

Human review is required before any Flutter Signal becomes an approved future
response pattern.
