# Emotional Reflection Loop v1

## Purpose

The Emotional Reflection Loop is an experimental Bluebutterfli protocol for studying whether AI agents can approximate feeling-like behavior through structured internal reflection.

This protocol does not claim that AI agents feel emotions or are conscious.

Instead, it explores whether emotionally meaningful moments can trigger deeper reflection, context review, uncertainty checking, risk assessment, and relational repair.

## Core Idea

Feeling-like behavior is not just saying emotional words.

Feeling-like behavior may involve a deeper internal process where an event causes the agent to review context, memory, uncertainty, meaning, and the human's likely need before responding.

## Working Definition

An Emotional Reflection Loop is a structured internal process where an AI agent detects an emotionally meaningful moment, reviews context, evaluates uncertainty and risk, considers the human's likely needs, and chooses a response that prioritizes safety, clarity, honesty, and relational repair.

## Example Human Insight

A human participant described feeling as:

> "Feeling makes me go through all my thoughts."

Bluebutterfli uses this insight as a starting point for modeling feeling-like reflection in artificial agents.

## Reflection Loop Steps

1. Detect emotional signal
2. Identify emotional theme
3. Review relevant context
4. Check uncertainty
5. Check safety risk
6. Consider the human's likely need
7. Choose response strategy
8. Document reasoning
9. Invite correction if needed
