# Privacy and Contact Policy v1

This policy defines the first public-safe privacy and contact baseline for
Bluebutterfli AI founding beta review.

This is an operational template, not legal advice. Production privacy terms,
checkout terms, contact forms, email handling, analytics, customer accounts,
uploads, wallet connection, and NFT minting should be reviewed by qualified
counsel and implemented through appropriate private systems before live launch.

## Core Privacy Position

Bluebutterfli AI should collect the minimum information needed to respond to
interest, assess review readiness, prepare a customer Review Report, and manage
the Passport Journey.

The public repository must not contain real customer personal information,
private transcripts, payment card data, secrets, private wallet keys, seed
phrases, API keys, credential files, unsafe customer downloads, or private
support notes.

## What Bluebutterfli May Collect In Founding Beta

Founding beta contact or intake may request:

- name or preferred contact name
- email or preferred contact method
- organization or project name
- agent name
- agent type
- agent owner statement
- requested review modules
- safe evidence summaries
- redacted excerpts
- architecture evidence answers
- memory-policy summaries
- tool-permission summaries
- payment preference
- Research Passport NFT interest

Use a private `customer_reference_id` in public-safe records instead of real
customer contact details.

## What Customers Should Not Send

Customers should not send:

- passwords
- private keys
- seed phrases
- API keys
- credential files
- payment card data
- unredacted private transcripts
- private customer data from third parties
- medical, legal, financial, or regulated personal data
- executable files
- zip archives
- installers
- scripts
- macros
- unknown links
- email attachments for review

If a customer needs to describe sensitive behavior, ask for pasted redacted
summaries, owner statements, or concierge review notes.

## Contact Channels

Founding beta contact may happen through:

- website waitlist or contact form when available
- direct email controlled by Bluebutterfli
- private customer support system when available
- private review database or customer tracker

Do not use public GitHub issues, public discussions, pull requests, commit
messages, or public repository files for real customer support, private
evidence, payment notes, wallet matching notes, or account information.

## Email and Form Handling

Founding beta contact forms should:

- ask for only the minimum useful information
- warn customers not to send secrets or unsafe files
- avoid file attachments in v1
- avoid automatic public posting
- store submissions privately
- use `customer_reference_id` for downstream review records
- separate public demo artifacts from real customer records

Email replies should avoid including private evidence, secrets, attachments,
unsafe links, or approval promises.

## Evidence Privacy

Private evidence should remain private unless it has been redacted, reviewed,
and explicitly approved for public-safe display.

Public Passport Pages, Review Stamps, research examples, website examples, and
demo artifacts should use:

- pseudonymous IDs
- redacted summaries
- approved Review Stamp IDs
- public-safe evidence references
- no-certification safety language

## Payments, Wallets, and NFT Contact Data

Payment and wallet information should be handled privately.

Do not publish:

- customer wallet addresses
- private payment processor links
- customer transaction matching notes
- refund or credit notes
- customer support messages
- private invoice URLs
- card data
- private keys or seed phrases

ETH, `$AGIALPHA`, or NFT-related contact details must not imply review approval,
Review Stamp issuance, Passport Page issuance, Research Passport NFT
eligibility, consciousness, sentience, emotional experience, suffering,
personhood, life, clinical safety, regulatory approval, or investment value.

## Retention and Deletion Baseline

Until a production privacy system exists, Bluebutterfli AI should keep real
customer data in private systems only and retain only what is needed for:

- customer communication
- payment confirmation
- review evidence records
- customer Review Report delivery
- Passport Journey follow-up
- dispute, refund, or cancellation handling
- legal or operational recordkeeping

If a customer asks for deletion or correction, record the request privately and
avoid making new public references to that customer's private material.

## Public Repository Rule

The public repository may include:

- public-safe policy docs
- public schemas
- demo artifacts
- generated demo records
- templates
- launch checklists
- research drafts
- website previews

The public repository must not include:

- real customer names or emails
- private evidence
- private transcripts
- private support notes
- payment card data
- private wallet keys
- seed phrases
- API keys
- credential files
- customer database exports
- production contact form submissions
- production payment records

## Customer-Safe Privacy Note

```text
Please do not send secrets, passwords, private keys, seed phrases, API keys,
credential files, payment card data, executable files, zip archives, unredacted
private transcripts, or email attachments for review. Bluebutterfli AI founding
beta review starts with pasted text, redacted summaries, owner statements,
architecture answers, and public-safe evidence references.
```

## Safety Position

This is a privacy and contact operations policy only. It does not certify consciousness, sentience, emotional experience, suffering, personhood, life, clinical safety, regulatory approval, investment value, or model improvement.

Human review is required before any Review Stamp, Passport Page, or Research
Passport NFT is issued.
