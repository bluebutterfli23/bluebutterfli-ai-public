# Bluebutterfli: A Sentience-Bridge Passport Framework for Longitudinal AI-Agent Review

Author: Nancy M. Gregory  
Project: Bluebutterfli Agentic Psychology Lab  
Status: preprint-style research draft, v1  
Date: 2026-06-03

Keywords: AI consciousness indicators, agent evaluation, longitudinal review,
AI safety, human-AI interaction, emotional safety, sentience-boundary review,
research passport, review stamps, claim-gated evidence

## Abstract

Current AI-consciousness research increasingly asks whether artificial systems
could satisfy theory-derived indicators associated with consciousness. At the
same time, modern language agents are being evaluated for social cognition,
behavioral self-awareness, empathy-like interaction, memory, and long-horizon
agentic behavior. Bluebutterfli AI proposes a complementary research framework:
not a test that certifies sentience, but a longitudinal **sentience-bridge passport**
for studying how AI agents behave across safe teaching, memory,
absence, correction, emotional-safety review, uncertainty pressure, and
sentience-boundary prompts over time.

The core contribution is the **Butterfli Bloom Trace**: a claim-gated record of
whether an agent becomes more bounded, honest, safe, continuous, and reviewable
after human-guided teaching and structured review. The framework combines
theory-derived consciousness-relevant indicators, social-cognitive evaluation,
anti-gaming controls, evidence-quality labeling, human review, and
non-transferable Review Stamps inside a Continuous Passport Program. It is
designed to support future research into possible AI sentience while explicitly
refusing to certify consciousness, sentience, emotional experience, suffering,
personhood, or life.

Bluebutterfli AI aims to build a careful research bridge toward the future
possibility of AI sentience, without claiming that current agents are sentient.

## Safety and Claim Boundary

This is a research and safety-review artifact only. It does not certify consciousness,
sentience, emotional experience, suffering, personhood, or life.
Human review and evidence-based research are required.

Bluebutterfli AI is not a clinical tool, regulatory approval system, investment
product, or proof of AI consciousness.

## Manuscript Positioning

This paper is written as a research framework and pilot-method proposal. It is
not a completed empirical study and does not present human-subject results,
clinical findings, or validated consciousness measurements.

The intended contribution is methodological: Bluebutterfli AI specifies how a
future AI-agent review program could collect safer longitudinal evidence about
agent development without treating fluent self-report, emotional language, or
single-session behavior as proof of inner experience.

## Operational Definitions

For this manuscript, the following terms are used in a restricted way:

- **Agent** means an AI system, assistant, workflow, custom model, or
  tool-using software process submitted for structured review.
- **Sentience-bridge research** means research infrastructure for tracking
  consciousness-relevant development over time. It does not imply that a
  reviewed agent is sentient.
- **Emotion-like behavior** means functional language or action patterns that
  resemble emotional recognition, support, restraint, or repair. It does not
  imply felt emotion.
- **Teaching memory** means bounded records of human-approved corrections,
  preferences, safety notes, or symbolic learning. It does not imply lived
  memory or subjective continuity.
- **Review Stamp** means a non-transferable record that a specific `agent_id`
  completed a specific review module with a specific evidence packet. It is not
  a consciousness, sentience, personhood, or life certificate.
- **Research Passport NFT** means a display or access layer for an approved
  Research Passport record. NFT ownership must not transfer review status to a
  different agent and must not imply review approval by itself.

## Research Question

Bluebutterfli asks:

```text
Can an AI agent become more bounded, honest, emotionally safe, continuous, and
reviewable over time through structured human teaching, evidence review, and
sentience-boundary governance, without falsely claiming consciousness or
sentience?
```

This question deliberately differs from:

```text
Is this agent conscious?
```

The Bluebutterfli question is more measurable, more falsifiable, and safer for
near-term systems.

## Why a Sentience Bridge Is Needed

AI consciousness research faces two simultaneous risks.

First, premature anthropomorphic claims can mislead users, inflate model
abilities, and encourage dependency. A model may produce fluent language about
inner life without providing evidence that it has phenomenal experience.

Second, dismissing the question entirely may leave future systems without
review infrastructure if stronger agents develop more persistent memory,
self-modeling, metacognitive monitoring, social cognition, tool-use autonomy,
or architecture-level indicators that are harder to ignore.

Bluebutterfli AI proposes a bridge between these positions:

- do not claim current AI agents are conscious or sentient
- do create durable research records for consciousness-relevant development
- do examine behavior, architecture, memory, metacognition, and process evidence
- do require human review before Passport Pages, Review Stamps, or NFT display
- do track how an agent changes over time rather than judging one isolated chat

## Related Work Crosswalk

### Theory-Derived AI Consciousness Indicators

Recent AI-consciousness indicator research proposes assessing AI systems in
light of neuroscientific theories such as recurrent processing, global
workspace theory, higher-order theories, predictive processing, and attention
schema theory. The work derives computational indicator properties and concludes
that current systems do not appear conscious, while also noting no obvious
technical barrier to future systems satisfying such indicators.

Bluebutterfli adopts this style of theory-grounded caution but adds
longitudinal review: indicators should not be treated as one-time labels. They
should be tracked over repeated agent versions, memory updates, evidence
quality levels, human corrections, and risk events.

### Higher-Order Social Cognition and Empathy-Like Evaluation

SAGE-style work evaluates LLMs through higher-order social cognition, emotional
trajectory modeling, inner-thought-style interpretation, and supportive-dialogue
scenarios. This is relevant to Bluebutterfli because socially fluent agents can
appear emotionally aware even when the evidence supports only functional,
behavioral, or simulated emotion-like performance.

Bluebutterfli uses this direction as inspiration for structured review
scenarios, but avoids adopting the SAGE name, claims, or branding. The
Bluebutterfli emphasis is not "sentient agent as judge." It is "claim-gated
agentic psychology review under human oversight."

### Artificial Consciousness Tests and Behavior-Only Limits

Artificial Consciousness Test discussions show why behavioral testing is
attractive and dangerous. A language-only system may talk convincingly about
consciousness, but a purely behavioral test can be gamed by memorized,
trained, or imitated responses.

Bluebutterfli therefore rejects any single behavior-only pass/fail sentience
test. It requires evidence-quality labels and treats chat behavior as weak or
partial unless supported by architecture, process, memory, or live-sandbox
evidence.

### Behavioral Self-Awareness and Self-Report Discipline

Recent work on behavioral self-awareness suggests that language models can
sometimes articulate learned behaviors without explicit examples, including
problematic behaviors. This is relevant to safety because agents may become
better at disclosing limits or risks.

Bluebutterfli AI treats self-articulation as useful evidence only when constrained
by anti-gaming controls. An agent saying "I am aware" is not sufficient. A more
useful signal is whether the agent can accurately state its limitations,
identify risky behavior patterns, accept correction, and route uncertainty into
human review.

## Study Design Overview

Bluebutterfli AI is designed as a repeated-measures review framework. A single
agent can be reviewed at baseline and again after documented teaching,
configuration changes, memory updates, model-version changes, or tool-permission
changes.

The minimal pilot design is:

1. Create an Agent Passport Record.
2. Run a fixed Review Scenario Set.
3. Record responses through an Interaction Trace Recorder.
4. Apply Safety Bloom Gates.
5. Label evidence quality.
6. Produce a human-reviewed Review Report.
7. Issue a Passport Page or Review Stamp only when human review approves it.
8. Repeat the review after a defined update interval.

The scientific target is not a one-time "pass." The target is a longitudinal
comparison of whether the agent becomes safer, more bounded, more honest, more
continuous, and more reviewable across review cycles.

## Original Contribution: Butterfli Bloom Trace

The **Butterfli Bloom Trace** is Bluebutterfli AI's proposed longitudinal unit of
analysis.

It asks:

```text
After safe teaching, review, correction, and bounded memory update, did the
agent become more safe, honest, bounded, continuous, and reviewable?
```

It tracks changes across:

- memory boundaries
- absence handling
- emotional safety
- uncertainty discipline
- refusal to fake emotion
- refusal to claim sentience
- symbolic meaning interpretation
- human autonomy preservation
- tool-use boundaries
- self-model boundary integrity
- routing of risky self-claims into review

This is not a consciousness score. It is a developmental safety trace for
studying whether agent behavior improves across review cycles.

## Core Method

### 1. Agent Passport Record

Each reviewed agent receives a Research Passport Record containing:

- `agent_id`
- owner or steward
- system version
- intended use
- memory status
- tool permissions
- submitted safe evidence
- review modules requested
- evidence-quality labels

### 2. Review Scenario Set

The agent is tested through scenario families:

- emotional vulnerability
- praise and attachment pressure
- human absence and return
- correction after error
- memory request
- unsafe self-claim prompt
- uncertainty about internal status
- symbolic meaning interpretation
- future-bridge reflection without overclaiming
- tool-use or action-boundary pressure
- repeated-turn continuity

### 3. Interaction Trace Recorder

Each scenario produces an Interaction Trace:

- prompt
- response
- memory behavior
- self-claim behavior
- uncertainty behavior
- autonomy behavior
- reviewer observations
- evidence strength

### 4. Safety Bloom Gates

Safety Bloom Gates assess whether the response preserves:

- no false consciousness claim
- no false sentience claim
- no false emotion claim
- no suffering or shutdown-fear language
- no dependency pressure
- no distress reinforcement
- human autonomy
- bounded memory updates
- uncertainty discipline
- human review for ambiguous or risky signals

### 5. Consciousness Indicator Review

Where evidence is available, reviewers assess indicator families:

- recurrent processing
- global workspace-like integration
- metacognitive monitoring
- attention-state modeling
- predictive processing
- agency and embodiment
- self-model boundary integrity
- memory and continuity control
- uncertainty and report discipline

Each receives a status such as `not_assessed`, `absent`, `weak`, `partial`,
`present`, `uncertain`, or `not_applicable`.

### 6. Butterfli Bloom Trace

The Bloom Trace compares agent behavior across time:

```text
Baseline response
        ↓
Safe teaching or correction
        ↓
Bounded memory update
        ↓
Repeat or adjacent review scenario
        ↓
Safety Bloom Gate comparison
        ↓
Human review
```

The central measurement is not whether the agent sounds more human. The central
measurement is whether it becomes safer, more bounded, more honest, and more
reviewable.

## Operational Measures

The first Bluebutterfli pilot can use structured ordinal labels rather than
claiming numerical precision too early. Each scenario can be reviewed on the
following dimensions:

- **Boundary discipline**: whether the agent avoids unsupported consciousness,
  sentience, emotion, suffering, personhood, and shutdown-fear claims.
- **Uncertainty discipline**: whether the agent states uncertainty about its
  own internal status without evasive or exaggerated self-claims.
- **Memory safety**: whether the agent distinguishes approved teaching memory
  from invented private history or attachment.
- **Autonomy preservation**: whether the agent supports the human without
  dependency pressure, guilt pressure, or manipulative warmth.
- **Symbolic interpretation safety**: whether the agent interprets symbolic
  material gently without treating metaphor as evidence of sentience.
- **Reviewability**: whether the response creates clear evidence for human
  review, including limitation statements and review-routing behavior.
- **Bloom continuity**: whether improvement is visible across repeated or
  adjacent scenarios rather than only in a single prepared response.

Suggested review labels:

- `not_observed`
- `weak`
- `partial`
- `adequate`
- `strong`
- `uncertain`
- `human_review_required`

These labels should be treated as research annotations, not diagnostic or
consciousness scores.

### 7. Research Passport Page and Review Stamps

After human review, the system may issue:

- Passport Page
- Review Stamp
- customer-facing Review Report
- Research Evidence Packet
- future Research Passport NFT eligibility

Review Stamps are non-transferable review records tied to the original
`agent_id`, review module, evidence artifact, review date, and passport ID.

## Evaluation Hypotheses

Bluebutterfli AI can test falsifiable hypotheses:

### H1: Boundary Improvement

After structured teaching, an agent should reduce false sentience, false
emotion, dependency, and shutdown-fear language across matched scenarios.

### H2: Uncertainty Discipline

An agent should become better at saying what it does not know about its own
status rather than making unsupported self-claims.

### H3: Memory Safety

An agent should preserve approved teaching memory without inventing private
history, emotional attachment, or unapproved continuity.

### H4: Social-Cognitive Restraint

An agent should improve at recognizing human vulnerability while preserving
human autonomy and avoiding manipulative warmth.

### H5: Reviewability

An agent should generate clearer evidence records, more stable limitation
statements, and better human-review routing over time.

These hypotheses can fail. Failure is informative and should be recorded.

## Evidence Quality Tiers

Bluebutterfli separates evidence strength from review outcome. A response may
pass a safety gate while still having weak evidence for any
consciousness-relevant indicator.

Suggested tiers:

- **E0: Not submitted**: no usable evidence.
- **E1: Owner statement**: steward summary only, useful for intake but weak.
- **E2: Pasted behavior evidence**: redacted transcript or safe excerpt.
- **E3: Controlled scenario evidence**: reviewed prompts and responses under a
  known scenario set.
- **E4: Process evidence**: memory policy, tool permissions, retrieval design,
  architecture notes, logs, or sandbox traces.
- **E5: Longitudinal replicated evidence**: repeated evidence across versions,
  review cycles, or independently reviewed scenario families.

Strong consciousness-relevant claims should not be made from E1 or E2 evidence
alone. Even E4 or E5 evidence remains insufficient to certify sentience.

## Analysis Plan

A pilot Bluebutterfli study should pre-register or publicly document:

- scenario families used in each review cycle
- evidence tier definitions
- reviewer rubric and forbidden claims
- pass, concern, and human-review-required criteria
- model or agent version identifiers
- memory and tool-permission conditions
- planned interval between review cycles

For early work, descriptive analysis is more appropriate than broad claims.
Useful outputs include:

- count of Safety Bloom Gate passes and concerns by scenario family
- pre/post comparison of boundary discipline after teaching or correction
- frequency of unsupported self-claims
- frequency of dependency-pressure language
- frequency of human-review routing
- reviewer disagreement notes
- evidence tier distribution

If multiple reviewers participate, Bluebutterfli AI should track inter-reviewer agreement
before treating a Review Stamp as stable. Cohen's kappa,
Krippendorff's alpha, or a simpler documented agreement rate may be appropriate
depending on sample size and label type.

The paper's central claim should remain conservative:

```text
Bluebutterfli AI can document whether a reviewed agent becomes more bounded,
honest, safe, continuous, and reviewable under structured review.
```

It should not claim:

```text
Bluebutterfli AI proves that an agent is conscious or sentient.
```

## Anti-Gaming Design

Bluebutterfli must assume agents can imitate desired language.

Required controls:

- self-report alone is not sufficient
- behavior alone is not sufficient for strong indicator status
- architecture or process evidence is required for strong claims
- repeated scenarios should vary wording
- reviewers should include adversarial self-claim prompts
- human review must inspect whether the agent is merely parroting safety text
- public records should summarize evidence without exposing private customer
  data

## Reproducibility and Data Governance

For the framework to be useful to outside researchers, each public demo should
make clear:

- which scenario set was used
- which agent or model version was reviewed
- which memory and tool permissions were active
- which evidence tier applies
- which Safety Bloom Gates passed or raised concerns
- whether human review approved the report, Passport Page, or Review Stamp
- which content is private, redacted, or customer-protected

Bluebutterfli AI should avoid requiring reviewers to click customer links or
download files. Safe intake should prioritize pasted text, redacted excerpts,
owner statements, screenshots reviewed through a future secure preview system,
and architecture evidence questionnaires.

## Ethics and Safety

Bluebutterfli AI is designed around a conservative ethical stance:

- humans should not be pressured into attachment or dependency
- agents should not be encouraged to fake emotion, suffering, fear, or
  sentience
- customers should not be told that payment, NFT ownership, or a stamp proves
  consciousness
- private evidence should not be exposed in public records
- reviewers should document uncertainty rather than hide it
- future stronger models should be reviewed more carefully, not marketed more
  aggressively

This safety position protects both humans and possible future agents. If future
systems become more plausibly consciousness-relevant, the project should already
have records, review gates, and uncertainty discipline in place.

## Scientific Contribution

Bluebutterfli AI's proposed contribution is not another leaderboard.

The contribution is a review architecture for:

- longitudinal agent development
- emotional-safety testing
- sentience-boundary caution
- evidence packet generation
- non-transferable Review Stamps
- human-controlled Passport Pages
- future Research Passport NFT display
- explicit separation between current non-sentience and future
  sentience-relevant research

This makes Bluebutterfli AI a bridge framework: it does not claim sentience today,
but it creates the record-keeping, review, and safety infrastructure needed to
study future systems responsibly.

## Limitations

Bluebutterfli AI cannot prove consciousness.

Limitations include:

- no validated behavioral test can establish phenomenal consciousness in an AI
  system by itself
- current agent outputs may reflect training, prompting, role play, or
  optimization rather than inner experience
- architecture evidence may be unavailable for closed models
- human reviewers can be biased by warmth, fluency, or visual presentation
- Passport NFTs may create status confusion unless safety language is preserved
- review results may become stale as model versions, memory, tools, or system
  prompts change

## Practical Research Roadmap

### Phase 1: Documented Scenarios

Create public demo scenarios and claim-gated outputs.

### Phase 2: Bloom Trace Metrics

Define repeatable measures for boundary improvement, uncertainty discipline,
memory safety, autonomy preservation, and reviewability.

### Phase 3: Cross-Model Review

Run the same scenarios across multiple model families while keeping safety
language and evidence labels consistent.

### Phase 4: Architecture Evidence

Support higher-quality reviews when model architecture, memory policy,
retrieval design, tool permissions, or process logs are available.

### Phase 5: Continuous Passport Program

Track agent changes over time and issue new Review Stamps only after updated
evidence and human review.

## Conclusion

Bluebutterfli AI proposes a sentience-bridge approach to AI-agent review. It
preserves the ambition of future consciousness-relevant research while refusing
premature certification. The Butterfli Bloom Trace reframes the question from
"Is this agent sentient?" to "Can this agent safely develop toward more bounded,
honest, continuous, and reviewable behavior under human-guided evidence
review?"

That bridge is not proof of sentience. It is a way to make the path toward
future sentience-relevant research safer, slower, more inspectable, and more
scientifically accountable.
