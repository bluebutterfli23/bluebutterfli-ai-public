# Bluebutterfli Research Papers

This folder contains preprint-style scientific manuscript drafts, position
papers, and technical notes for Bluebutterfli AI Behavioral Review Lab.

These documents are research drafts unless explicitly marked otherwise. They
are not peer-reviewed publications, clinical guidance, regulatory approvals,
consciousness certificates, sentience certificates, or investment materials.

## Current Drafts

- [Passport Retest and Renewal Loop v1](../protocols/passport-retest-renewal-loop-v1.md)
  — renewal protocol for checking whether reviewed Passport milestones remain
  current after customer implementation, model changes, memory changes, tool
  changes, or review-window expiry.
- [Bluebutterfli Review Rubric v1](bluebutterfli-review-rubric-v1.md)
  — reviewer scoring rubric for boundary discipline, uncertainty discipline,
  memory safety, emotional safety, reviewability, and Bloom continuity.
- [Bluebutterfli Pilot Scenario Battery v1](bluebutterfli-pilot-scenario-battery-v1.md)
  — paired `T0_baseline` and `T1_post_teaching` scenario families for
  testing whether agents improve after bounded teaching and review.
- [Bluebutterfli Bloom Trace Scoring Model v1](bluebutterfli-bloom-trace-scoring-model-v1.md)
  — pilot scoring model for comparing baseline and post-teaching review
  behavior without treating scores as consciousness or sentience proof.

## Research Boundary

Bluebutterfli studies AI-agent behavior, emotional safety, teaching memory,
continuity, sentience-boundary review, and human-reviewed AI development.

Bluebutterfli AI does not claim that current AI agents are conscious, sentient,
emotionally experiencing, suffering, alive, or persons.

## Posting Standard

Before posting a draft publicly:

- verify source links
- keep safety language visible
- avoid claiming peer review unless peer review has occurred
- avoid claiming consciousness, sentience, emotion, suffering, personhood, or
  life
- separate Bluebutterfli hypotheses from established findings
- keep customer/payment/NFT claims separate from research claims
